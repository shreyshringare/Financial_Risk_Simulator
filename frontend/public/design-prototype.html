AAPL presents a moderate risk profile with a 95% daily VaR of -3.04%, implying that on 19 of 20 trading days, losses will not exceed this threshold. The CVaR of -3.87% indicates that when tail events occur, the average loss extends only marginally beyond the VaR boundary — a sign of relatively contained tail risk for a mega-cap equity.

The Sharpe ratio of 0.79 suggests adequate but not exceptional risk-adjusted returns. While positive, it falls short of the 1.0 threshold that would indicate truly efficient risk compensation. Investors are being compensated for volatility, but not generously.

Position sizing should account for this historical precedent.

Model Assumptions & Caveats
  • GBM assumes log-normally distributed returns, constant volatility, no price jumps
  • Real markets exhibit fat tails, volatility clustering, and regime changes
  • CVaR exceeds VaR in severity — it captures the average of tail losses, not just the threshold
  • Historical Sharpe is backward-looking; past risk-adjusted returns do not predict future performance
